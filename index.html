Texas International College Student Murdered by Classmate

A shocking crime has rocked the community of Texas International College in Shifal, Kathmandu, where a 20-year-old student was arrested for allegedly murdering a fellow classmate. The incident occurred late last night when the two students got into an argument that escalated quickly. Witnesses reported hearing screams coming from the dormitory before the police arrived.

The victim, whose identity has not yet been disclosed, was known for being an active member of the college's student council. Friends and faculty members expressed their disbelief over the tragic news, describing the victim as a kind and dedicated student.

Authorities have stated that the suspect was taken into custody without incident and is being held for questioning. The police are investigating the circumstances surrounding the murder, including any potential motives. This incident has raised serious concerns about student safety on campus, prompting the administration to increase security measures.

The college is providing counseling services to students affected by this tragedy and is urging anyone with information to come forward.

Crime Rates Soar in Nepal: A Growing Concern

Crime rates in Nepal have been steadily increasing, with recent statistics showing a worrying trend in urban areas. According to the latest report from the Nepal Police, thefts, assaults, and drug-related offenses have surged by over 30% in the past year alone.

Experts attribute this rise to various factors, including economic challenges, unemployment, and a lack of effective law enforcement. Community leaders are calling for immediate action to address the root causes of this troubling trend, urging the government to invest in crime prevention programs and support services.
